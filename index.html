Institutional Profile & Editorial Charter

Crypto Financial Times (CFT) is a leading independent provider of high-fidelity digital asset intelligence. Established in early 2024, our mission is to provide professional-grade market analysis for global wealth managers, family offices, and institutional participants navigating the decentralized economy.

Our editorial framework is built upon three core pillars: Data Integrity, Regulatory Clarity, and Macro-Economic Context. In an environment often clouded by volatility and fragmented information, CFT serves as a stabilizing terminal of record. We specialize in the convergence of Traditional Finance (TradFi) and Decentralized Finance (DeFi), offering deep-dive technical analysis on network security, liquid staking derivatives, and the tokenization of Real-World Assets (RWAs).

The CFT research team consists of veteran financial analysts with backgrounds in quantitative trading and global macro-strategy. We maintain strict independence from the protocols we cover, ensuring that our "Institutional Asset Intelligence" remains an unbiased source of truth for the global investment community.

Data Privacy & Transparency Report

Last Comprehensive Audit: March 19, 2026

At Crypto Financial Times, user privacy is integrated into our technical architecture. This Transparency Report details how we manage information to maintain a secure and professional environment for our readers.

1. Advertising and Third-Party Interactions: We utilize Google AdSense as our primary advertising partner. Google and its partner sites use cookies to serve ads based on your visits to this and other websites on the internet. These "DART cookies" allow for a personalized professional experience. You may opt-out of personalized advertising by visiting the Google Ad Settings page.

2. Log File Integrity: Like all professional financial terminals, we follow a standard procedure of using log files. These files log visitors when they visit websites. The information collected includes internet protocol (IP) addresses, browser type, Internet Service Provider (ISP), date and time stamps, and referring/exit pages.
